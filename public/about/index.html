About

Hey, I’m Derrek

I’m a System Administration Manager at SAIC with over a decade of experience keeping complex infrastructure running — from Navy shipboard IT to enterprise mission systems. I’ve spent my career in the deep end of the tech stack, and now I’m deliberately pushing into cloud engineering, AI/ML, and data science.

This site is where I build in public. Every project, writeup, and lesson learned from that transition lives here.

Background

I got my start in IT serving in the U.S. Navy from 2013 to 2017, where I learned quickly that systems don’t care what time it is when they break. That foundation of operational discipline and problem-solving under pressure has shaped how I approach every technical challenge since.

After separating, I earned a BA from the University of Iowa and moved into the civilian IT world, working my way into systems administration and eventually management. Today at SAIC I manage systems and teams supporting mission-critical environments.

What I Work With

Over the years I’ve gotten my hands dirty with a wide range of technologies:

  • Operating Systems & Virtualization: Windows Server, Red Hat Linux, Solaris, VMware
  • Scripting & Automation: PowerShell
  • Networking & Security: Cisco (CCNA), CEH-level security practices
  • Cloud: AWS, Microsoft Azure
  • Mission Systems: Specialized DoD and enterprise mission-critical environments

Where I’m Headed

I’ve spent years making sure systems stay up. Now I’m focused on building on top of them.

My current focus is developing hands-on skills in Microsoft Azure, Python, AI/ML, and data engineering — and documenting every step of that journey here. The goal is to bridge the operational depth of a seasoned sysadmin with the engineering skills needed to build intelligent, cloud-native systems.

If you’re on a similar path or want to connect, find me on GitHub or LinkedIn.
